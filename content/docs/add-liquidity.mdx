## What this tool does

FORGE Pool lets you create trading pools for your token on Solana DEXs, add liquidity, and remove it — all from your wallet without navigating each DEX's interface separately.

## What it costs

| Action | FORGE fee | DEX setup cost |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Create pool (per DEX) | 0.1 SOL | 0.4–0.6 SOL (paid to DEX) |
| Add liquidity to existing pool | 0.05 SOL | Solana gas (~0.001 SOL) |
| Remove liquidity | 0.05 SOL | Solana gas (~0.001 SOL) |

DEX setup costs are pass-throughs paid directly to the DEX protocol. They vary and are shown in the cost summary before you sign. Fees can change.

## Before you start

- Token already created (you need the mint address)
- SOL for liquidity deposit + FORGE fee + DEX setup fee
- Tokens to deposit (in your wallet)
- Decision on which DEX(s) to use

## Step-by-step: Create a pool

### Step 1 — Select your token

Go to FORGE → Pool. Paste your token mint address or select it from the dropdown if you recently created it.

### Step 2 — Choose a DEX

Click the DEX you want to create a pool on. Supported DEXs: Raydium, Orca, Meteora, PumpSwap, Invariant, FluxBeam. You can select multiple.

### Step 3 — Set deposit amounts

For each DEX, enter:
- **Token amount** — how many of your tokens to deposit
- **SOL amount** — how much SOL to pair with them

The ratio determines the initial price. Example: 1,000,000 tokens + 10 SOL = 0.00001 SOL per token.

### Step 4 — Review cost and sign

The cost summary shows the FORGE fee (0.1 SOL per pool) plus the DEX setup cost. Review and approve in your wallet.

## Step-by-step: Add to existing pool

### Step 1 — Go to Pool → Add

Select your token. FORGE will detect existing pools for your token.

### Step 2 — Enter amount

Enter how much SOL (or token) to add. The interface shows the current pool price so you can match the ratio.

### Step 3 — Sign

Approve in your wallet. LP tokens will appear in your wallet.

## Step-by-step: Remove liquidity

### Step 1 — Go to Pool → Remove

Select your token and the pool you want to remove from.

### Step 2 — Set percentage

Choose what percentage of your LP position to remove (25%, 50%, 100%, or custom).

### Step 3 — Sign

Approve and receive your underlying tokens and SOL back.

## What happens on-chain

Pool creation calls the DEX's on-chain program (e.g., Raydium's AMM or CPMM program) to initialize a new pool account, set the initial price, and deposit your liquidity. You receive LP tokens representing your share. These are standard Solana transactions — verifiable on Solscan.

<Warning>
**Liquidity is at risk.** Adding liquidity exposes you to impermanent loss — the value of your position may change relative to holding the tokens separately. This is a DeFi mechanic, not a FORGE behavior. Understand it before committing large amounts.
</Warning>
