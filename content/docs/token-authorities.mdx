## What are token authorities?

When you create a Solana token, three special permissions are assigned to your wallet by default:

| Authority | What it can do |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Mint authority** | Create new tokens (increase total supply) |
| **Freeze authority** | Freeze individual token accounts (prevent transfers) |
| **Update authority** | Change on-chain metadata (name, symbol, logo URI) |

These exist for legitimate reasons: you might need to mint tokens in phases, or update your logo after launch. But they also represent risks to holders — someone with mint authority could dilute supply indefinitely.

## Why DEX scanners care

Tools like DEXScreener, Rugcheck, Birdeye, and RugDoc scan token authorities as part of their safety ratings. A token with active mint authority gets flagged because the creator could theoretically print unlimited tokens. Revoking authorities removes these flags and signals to potential buyers that the supply is final.

## Mint authority

**Active:** The owner can call `mintTo` at any time, increasing total supply and diluting all existing holders.

**Revoked:** The total supply is permanently capped at its current value. No one — not even you — can ever mint more tokens.

**When to revoke:** After your initial supply distribution is complete. If you have a vesting or staking contract that mints rewards, keep mint authority until that contract is deployed and funded.

<Warning>
**Revoking mint authority is permanent.** After revocation, the supply is locked forever. Ensure your total distribution (team, investors, community, liquidity) is complete before revoking.
</Warning>

## Freeze authority

**Active:** The owner can freeze any token holder's account, preventing them from transferring or selling their tokens.

**Revoked:** No account can ever be frozen by anyone.

**When to revoke:** For most community tokens, revoke at creation. Keeping freeze authority can be perceived as a control mechanism that lets you block specific wallets from selling.

## Update authority (make immutable)

**Active:** The owner can change on-chain metadata: name, symbol, and the metadata URI (which points to the logo and description).

**Revoked (immutable):** Metadata is permanently locked. The name, symbol, and logo will never change.

**Cost:** Free — making a token immutable costs nothing beyond Solana gas.

## How to revoke authorities

### At creation
Enable the options in Step 5 of the Create Token flow. Cost: +0.05 SOL each for mint and freeze. Make immutable is free.

### After creation

Go to **FORGE Tools** and select:
- **Revoke Mint** to revoke mint authority
- **Revoke Freeze** to revoke freeze authority
- **Make Immutable** to revoke update authority

Each action requires your wallet to sign a transaction confirming you hold the current authority.

## What shows on DEX scanners

After revoking:

| Scan result | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| ✅ Mint disabled | Mint authority revoked |
| ✅ Freeze disabled | Freeze authority revoked |
| ✅ Immutable | Update authority revoked |

Tokens with all three revoked receive the highest trust ratings on most scanners.
