Once you've created a Raydium pool, you'll often need to add more liquidity as your project grows, or eventually remove it. This guide covers how to do both using Solana Token, plus what to understand about LP tokens before you act.

## Understanding LP tokens first

Every time you add liquidity to a Raydium CPMM pool, you receive **LP tokens** in return. These tokens:
- Represent your proportional share of the pool
- Earn a share of trading fees generated in the pool
- Must be held (not burned) if you want to be able to remove liquidity later

**Critical:** If LP tokens are burned, the liquidity is permanently locked. LP burning is a trust signal (the creator can't rug), but it means you can never get that liquidity back. Treat burning LP tokens as a one-way door.

## How to add liquidity to an existing pool

This is useful when:
- You want to increase depth after your token gains traction
- You have accumulated more tokens and want to put them to work
- You want to reduce price impact for larger trades

### Step 1 — Go to Solana Token → Pool → Add

Navigate to the Pool section and select **Add liquidity**.

### Step 2 — Find your pool

Enter your token mint address. Solana Token will detect your existing pool(s) and show the current price and pool depth.

### Step 3 — Enter your deposit amount

You can enter either the SOL amount or the token amount — the other side auto-fills based on the current pool price. You cannot set your own ratio when adding to an existing pool; you must match the current price.

### Step 4 — Sign

Solana Token charges a flat 0.05 SOL plus Solana gas (~0.001 SOL). Approve the transaction and receive additional LP tokens.

## How to remove liquidity

Removing liquidity closes or reduces your position, returning the underlying tokens and SOL.

### Step 1 — Go to Solana Token → Pool → Remove

Navigate to Pool → Remove.

### Step 2 — Select your token and pool

Choose your token and the pool you want to remove from. Solana Token shows your current LP position and the current value of your share.

### Step 3 — Choose percentage to remove

Select 25%, 50%, 100%, or enter a custom percentage.

### Step 4 — Review and sign

The preview shows how much SOL and tokens you will receive. This includes any trading fees your position has earned. Sign the transaction and receive your assets.

## What you receive when removing liquidity

You receive your share of the pool at the current pool ratio, not the ratio when you deposited. If the price has changed since you added liquidity:
- If your token price went up relative to SOL, you'll receive fewer tokens and more SOL
- If your token price went down relative to SOL, you'll receive more tokens and less SOL

This is how all AMM pools work. The trading fees you earned partially offset any impermanent loss.

## Timing considerations

- **Don't remove liquidity immediately** if you just added it — there's no time lock for CPMM, but removing right after adding accomplishes nothing
- **During high volatility**, the pool ratio shifts faster. If you need to remove, the amount you receive changes with every trade
- **Fee earnings** accumulate over time — the longer your position is active, the more fees you earn

<Warning>
**Removing all liquidity makes your token untradeable** on that DEX. If this is the only pool for your token, buyers will have no way to purchase it after removal. Make sure you understand the market impact before removing.
</Warning>
