Token burns are one of the most discussed mechanics in Solana projects. This guide explains what burning actually does on-chain, the difference between burning tokens and burning LP tokens, and how to do each with Solana Token.

## What burning does on-chain

When you burn SPL tokens, the transaction calls the Token program's `burn` instruction, which:

1. Deducts the burned amount from your token account balance
2. Reduces the mint's total supply counter by the same amount

The tokens are not sent to a dead wallet — they are destroyed. The total supply shown on Solscan and all explorers decreases immediately after the transaction confirms.

## What burning does and doesn't do

**Does:**
- Reduce total supply permanently
- Reduce circulating supply (if burned tokens were in circulation)
- Show a decreased supply on all explorers
- Signal commitment to token economics if done publicly

**Does not:**
- Guarantee price appreciation
- Increase your market cap (market cap = price × supply, burning reduces supply but buyers/sellers determine price)
- Remove tokens from other holders' wallets
- Affect uncirculated tokens you haven't minted yet

## When burning makes sense

**After a vesting unlock:** If you minted tokens for marketing or team allocations that were never distributed, burning the unused portion signals that those tokens won't hit the market.

**Supply reduction commitment:** Some projects commit to burning a percentage of transaction fees or treasury tokens on a schedule. This is a legitimate tokenomics mechanic if disclosed upfront.

**Correction of a minting error:** If you accidentally minted too many tokens, burning the excess corrects it.

**When it doesn't make sense:** Burning small amounts of tokens for a temporary price boost is transparent and rarely effective. Burning creates lasting value only when it's meaningful (a large percentage of supply) and part of a consistent, publicly communicated policy.

## How to burn tokens with Solana Token

### Step 1 — Go to Solana Token → Burn

Navigate to [solanatoken.app/burn](/burn).

### Step 2 — Select your token

Choose the token you want to burn from the dropdown. Your current balance is shown. You can burn any SPL token you hold, not just tokens you created.

### Step 3 — Enter the amount

Type the number of tokens to burn. Click **Max** to burn your entire balance. The interface shows:
- Amount to burn
- Remaining supply after burn

### Step 4 — Confirm and sign

<Warning>
**This action is permanent.** Check the confirmation box that acknowledges burning is irreversible. Verify the amount one final time. Click **Burn tokens** and approve in your wallet.
</Warning>

Cost: Free (no Solana Token fee). Gas: ~0.0001 SOL.

## How to burn LP tokens

Burning LP tokens is a separate but related action. LP tokens represent your share of a DEX liquidity pool. Burning them permanently removes your ability to withdraw that liquidity.

**Why burn LP tokens?**
It signals to buyers that the liquidity is permanently locked — the creator cannot do a "rug pull" (removing all liquidity and crashing the price). Many DEX scanners specifically check for burned LP tokens.

### Step 1 — Go to Solana Token → Tools → Burn LP

Navigate to the Burn LP tool in the Tools menu.

### Step 2 — Select your token and pool

Choose your token and the specific pool whose LP tokens you want to burn.

### Step 3 — Enter amount or burn all

You can burn a percentage or all of your LP tokens. Most projects burn 100% as a trust signal.

### Step 4 — Confirm and sign

<Warning>
**Burning LP tokens is permanent.** Once burned, you cannot withdraw the underlying liquidity. This is a one-way trust commitment. Only do this if you intend for the liquidity to remain in the pool permanently.
</Warning>

## What DEX scanners show after burning

After burning LP tokens, DEX scanners like Rugcheck and DEXScreener will typically update their liquidity safety indicator:

> ✅ Liquidity locked — LP tokens burned (permanent lock)

This is considered one of the strongest safety signals because it removes the ability to rug-pull.
