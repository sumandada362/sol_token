Making a token immutable on Solana means revoking its **update authority** — the on-chain permission that controls whether metadata can be changed. After revocation, the token's name, symbol, and metadata URI are permanently locked. No one, including you, can ever change them.

## What update authority controls

Every Solana token created with the Metaplex metadata standard has an update authority — an account (usually your wallet) that can call `updateMetadataAccountV2` to change:
- Token name
- Token symbol
- Metadata URI (the URL pointing to your logo, description, and social links)

If this authority is active, the holder can change any of these at any time.

## Why immutability matters

**For buyers and holders:**
An immutable token cannot be renamed, rebranded, or have its logo swapped out. What you see is what it is. This matters because a bad actor with active update authority could, theoretically, change a token's metadata to impersonate another project.

**For DEX scanners:**
Most scanner tools show whether a token is mutable or immutable. An immutable token gets a green checkmark. A mutable token gets a flag (though it's considered a lower-severity risk compared to active mint authority).

**For your own credibility:**
Locking your metadata publicly signals that you are committed to your brand and are not planning to change course.

## What immutability does NOT lock

- **Mint authority** — separate, controls supply
- **Freeze authority** — separate, controls freezing
- **LP tokens** — immutability doesn't affect pool liquidity
- **Off-chain metadata content** — if your URI points to a mutable endpoint, that content can still change (see FAQ)

## When to make your token immutable

If your metadata (name, symbol, logo, description) is finalized, make it immutable as soon as possible. Most projects do this at creation. The only reason to wait is if you know you still need to update your logo or social links after launch.

If you already made metadata changes and you're now happy with it, revoke update authority to lock it in.

## How to make a token immutable with FORGE

### Option 1: At creation

In Step 5 of the FORGE Create Token flow, enable **Make Immutable**. This is free and included in the single creation transaction.

### Option 2: After creation

### Step 1 — Go to FORGE → Tools → Make Immutable

Navigate to the Make Immutable tool.

### Step 2 — Connect your wallet

Connect the wallet that currently holds update authority. FORGE verifies this automatically.

### Step 3 — Select your token

Paste your mint address or select from the dropdown.

### Step 4 — Confirm and sign

<Warning>
**This is permanent.** After revoking update authority, the metadata can never be changed — by anyone. Verify your token name, symbol, and logo are correct before proceeding.
</Warning>

Check the confirmation box and click **Make immutable**. Approve in your wallet. Cost: Free (Solana gas only, ~0.0001 SOL).

## Verify on Solscan

After the transaction confirms:
1. Go to solscan.io and search your mint address
2. Open the Metadata tab
3. The "Update Authority" field should show `null` or "Revoked"
