Before buying any Solana token, or to understand how your own token will be evaluated by buyers, you should know how to read the key on-chain safety signals. This guide walks through what to check and what each indicator actually means.

## The four things that actually matter

Most Solana token safety checks come down to four factors:

1. **Mint authority status** — Can new tokens be printed?
2. **Freeze authority status** — Can holder wallets be frozen?
3. **Holder concentration** — How many wallets control how much supply?
4. **Liquidity status** — Is the liquidity accessible to the creator or locked?

Everything else (social links, website, team claims) is off-chain and unverifiable through code.

---

## 1. Mint authority

**What it is:** The on-chain permission to create (mint) more tokens.

**Active (orange flag):** The creator or whoever holds mint authority can call `mintTo` and increase the total supply at any time. If you hold tokens in a project with active mint authority, your percentage of supply is not guaranteed.

**Revoked (green):** The total supply is permanently capped. No one, including the creator, can ever mint more.

**Where to check:** Solscan → Token info → Mint Authority field. Or use Solana Token Analytics — mint authority shows as a badge.

**How to interpret:**
- Active mint authority = the supply claim is marketing, not code
- Revoked mint authority = the supply is actually fixed

---

## 2. Freeze authority

**What it is:** The on-chain permission to freeze individual token accounts, preventing the holder from transferring or selling.

**Active (orange flag):** The authority holder can freeze any wallet's token balance. This is a potential mechanism to block selling.

**Revoked (green):** No wallet can ever be frozen. Token transfers are always permissioned by the holder, not a third party.

**Note:** Many legitimate projects have freeze authority active. It's not automatically a red flag — but it's a power that's rarely necessary for a public community token.

---

## 3. Holder concentration

**What it is:** The percentage of total supply held by the top N wallets.

**Why it matters:** High concentration means a small number of wallets could dump a significant portion of supply simultaneously, causing a sharp price decline.

| Top 10 wallets hold | Interpretation |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Under 20% | Well-distributed |
| 20–50% | Moderate concentration |
| 50–80% | High — significant sell pressure risk |
| Over 80% | Very high — single-wallet movements dominate price |

**Important context:** The creator wallet and any locked vesting contracts count toward concentration. A token where 50% is held by a vesting contract with a 3-year lock is very different from 50% held by a single wallet with no restrictions.

**Where to check:** Solana Token Analytics → Holder distribution panel. Also Solscan → Token Holders.

---

## 4. Liquidity status

**What it is:** Whether trading liquidity exists, and whether it can be removed.

**No liquidity:** The token cannot be traded on a DEX. You can transfer it peer-to-peer, but there's no market price.

**Liquidity present, LP held by wallet:** The pool exists and trades are possible. But the wallet holding LP tokens can remove all liquidity at any time — this is the classic mechanism for a "rug pull." Price goes to near zero when liquidity is removed.

**LP burned:** The most positive signal. LP tokens have been sent to the burn address. The liquidity is permanently locked in the pool. A rug pull via liquidity removal is not possible (though a token can still fail for other reasons).

## How to check with Solana Token Analytics

Navigate to your token's analytics page at `solanatoken.dravyo.com/analytics/[mint-address]` or through your token page.

You'll see:
- **Authority badges** — color-coded mint, freeze, and update authority status
- **Holder chart** — top holders and distribution graph
- **Concentration gauge** — top 10 holder percentage
- **Pool info** — linked DEX pools with LP status

---

## How to cross-reference with external scanners

For a thorough check, also run:

**Rugcheck.xyz**
Paste the mint address. Shows a composite risk score plus individual flags. Good for a quick snapshot.

**DEXScreener**
Search your token. Click the token name to see the info panel. Shows LP lock status, top holder distribution, and links to the Solscan token page.

**Birdeye**
Detailed analytics including holder history over time, which can reveal if a wallet has been slowly accumulating before a potential dump.

---

## What these checks cannot tell you

- Whether the project will succeed
- Whether the team will follow through on their roadmap
- Whether the social media presence is authentic
- Whether the website will still exist next month

On-chain safety checks are necessary but not sufficient. A clean on-chain profile removes specific technical risks but does not make a token a good investment.
