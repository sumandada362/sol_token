When you create a Solana token, two permissions are assigned to your wallet: **mint authority** (the right to create more tokens) and **freeze authority** (the right to freeze holder wallets). For most public tokens, these should be revoked.

This guide explains what these authorities mean, why they appear on DEX scanners, and exactly how to revoke them.

## What mint authority does

Mint authority allows the holder to call `mintTo` — the on-chain instruction that creates new tokens. If someone holds mint authority, they can print more tokens at any time with no upper limit other than what's in the program logic.

For a public token, active mint authority means:
- The supply is not capped
- The creator can dilute all existing holders at any time
- There is no guarantee that "total supply" means anything

This is why it gets flagged.

## What freeze authority does

Freeze authority allows the holder to call `freezeAccount` — which prevents a specific wallet from transferring, selling, or otherwise moving their token balance. 

For a public token, active freeze authority means:
- The creator can freeze any holder's wallet
- A buyer could be locked out of their own tokens
- The authority holder has a form of control over the secondary market

This gets flagged as a potential control mechanism.

## What DEX scanners actually check

Tools like DEXScreener, Rugcheck, Birdeye, and RugDoc query the Solana token program directly to read the mint account's authority fields. If either is set to a non-null public key, that authority is active.

Typical scanner output for an unrevoked token:

> ⚠️ Mint authority active — creator can mint unlimited tokens  
> ⚠️ Freeze authority active — creator can freeze holder wallets  

After revoking:

> ✅ Mint disabled — supply is permanently capped  
> ✅ Freeze disabled — no wallet can be frozen  

Most scanners treat both revocations as positive trust signals. Some aggregate them into a safety score.

## When to revoke

### Mint authority

Revoke after your initial supply distribution is fully done. If you have:
- Vesting contracts that mint on a schedule → wait until those are funded or deployed
- Staking rewards that mint new tokens → wait until the staking contract is set up
- Any planned future minting → wait until it's complete

If your supply is already what it should be, revoke now. Every day it's not revoked, someone could question your intentions.

### Freeze authority

Revoke as soon as possible for most community or meme tokens. Unless you have a specific regulatory or compliance reason to retain freeze authority (e.g., a regulated stablecoin), there is no good reason to keep it active.

<Warning>
**Revoking is permanent.** You cannot restore either authority after revoking. Make sure all supply distribution and any authority-dependent operations are complete.
</Warning>

## How to revoke mint authority on Solana Token

### Step 1 — Go to Solana Token → Tools → Revoke Mint

Navigate to [solanatoken.dravyo.com/tools/revoke-mint](/tools/revoke-mint).

### Step 2 — Connect your wallet

Connect the wallet that holds the current mint authority. Solana Token will verify this automatically.

### Step 3 — Select your token

Paste your mint address or choose from the dropdown. Solana Token displays the current authority status.

### Step 4 — Confirm and sign

Check the irreversibility confirmation box. Click **Revoke mint authority**. Approve in your wallet.

Cost: **0.05 SOL** Solana Token fee + gas.

## How to revoke freeze authority on Solana Token

The process is identical:

1. Go to [solanatoken.dravyo.com/tools/revoke-freeze](/tools/revoke-freeze)
2. Connect your authority wallet
3. Select your token
4. Confirm and sign

Cost: **0.05 SOL** Solana Token fee + gas.

You can also do both during token creation (in Step 4 of the Create Token flow) — which saves you two separate transactions later.

## What about update authority (make immutable)?

Update authority controls whether you can change on-chain metadata. Revoking it makes your token **immutable** — the name, symbol, and logo URI will never change.

This is free (no Solana Token fee, just gas). Use [solanatoken.dravyo.com/tools/make-immutable](/tools/make-immutable).

## After revoking: verify on-chain

After the transaction confirms, check your token on Solscan:
1. Go to solscan.io and search your mint address
2. Click the token name → see the "Token Info" tab
3. Look for "Mint Authority" and "Freeze Authority" fields — they should show `null` or "Revoked"

You can also use the Solana Token Analytics page for your token — the authority badges will update green within a few minutes.
