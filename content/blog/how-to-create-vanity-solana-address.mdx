Most Solana token mint addresses look like: `7xKqAbcDefGhiJkLmnOpQrStUvWxYz1234567890ab`. A vanity address lets you choose a prefix so it looks like: `Solana TokenAbcDefGhiJkLmnOpQrStUvWxYz1234567890ab`.

This is a cosmetic feature — it doesn't affect security or token mechanics — but it can make your token's address instantly recognizable to your community.

## How vanity address generation works

Every Solana address is derived from a keypair (a public/private key pair). To generate a vanity address, a tool generates thousands of keypairs per second until it finds one whose public key starts with your desired prefix.

This is called "grinding" and it runs locally in your browser — no keys leave your device. The private key of the generated keypair becomes the mint's keypair (not your wallet keypair — your wallet stays unchanged).

The time required grows exponentially with prefix length:

| Prefix length | Approximate time |
|---------------|-----------------|
| 3 characters | < 1 second |
| 4 characters | Seconds to 1 minute |
| 5 characters | 1–60 minutes |
| 6 characters | Hours |
| 7+ characters | Days or more |

These are rough estimates. The actual time is random — you might find a match immediately or it might take longer than the estimate.

## What characters are valid

Solana addresses use **base58 encoding**, which excludes characters that look visually similar:
- No `0` (zero)
- No `O` (capital o)
- No `I` (capital i)
- No `l` (lowercase L)

Valid characters: `1-9 A-H J-N P-Z a-k m-z`

So you can't have an address starting with "FULL" or "LION" because of the `l` and `I`. You can have "Solana Token", "MOON", "BULL", etc.

## Case sensitivity

Solana addresses are case-sensitive. "moon" and "MOON" are different — and some character combinations in mixed case may be harder to grind than others.

## How to create a vanity token address with Solana Token

### Step 1 — Open Solana Token Create Token

Go to [solanatoken.app/create-token](/create-token).

### Step 2 — Fill in your token details

Complete all the standard fields: name, symbol, supply, decimals, logo, and authority preferences.

### Step 3 — Enable vanity address in Advanced options

In the Advanced section, check **Vanity address**. A text input appears for your desired prefix.

### Step 4 — Enter your prefix

Type the prefix you want (e.g., "Solana Token"). Keep it to 4–5 characters unless you're prepared to wait a long time.

The UI shows an estimated generation time for your chosen prefix.

### Step 5 — Wait for grinding

Click **Generate**. The browser begins grinding keypairs locally. Progress is shown in real-time (keypairs per second, estimated time remaining). Do not close the browser tab during this process.

For longer prefixes, you can start the grind and let it run in the background.

### Step 6 — Review and sign

Once a matching address is found, the mint address is locked in. Review your token details and sign as normal.

## Security note

The grind happens locally in your browser. The keypair generated is used only for the token's mint account — not as a wallet keypair. Your wallet's private key is never involved in the grind process.

## Is a vanity address worth it?

**Worth it if:**
- You want your token's address to be immediately recognizable
- Your community will share the mint address often and recognition matters
- You're okay waiting for a 4–5 character prefix

**Not worth it if:**
- You want a very long prefix (7+ characters could take days)
- Time-to-launch is critical
- Your community identifies the token by name, not address

Most projects skip vanity addresses. They're a nice touch but not a significant factor in success.
